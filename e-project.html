Football (Soccer)

Team Sport

Rules

  • 11 players per team on the field
  • 90 minutes match (2 halves of 45 min)
  • No hands except goalkeeper in penalty area
  • Offside rule applies
  • Yellow and red card disciplinary system

Strategy

  • Formation tactics (4-4-2, 4-3-3, 3-5-2)
  • Possession-based vs counter-attack
  • Set-piece execution
  • Pressing and defensive organization
  • Wing play and crossing strategies

Facts & History

  • Modern rules established in England (1863)
  • Most popular sport globally
  • FIFA World Cup every 4 years since 1930
  • Over 250 million players worldwide
  • UEFA Champions League is top club competition

Key Tournaments

  • FIFA World Cup
  • UEFA Champions League
  • English Premier League
  • Copa America
  • UEFA European Championship

Statistics

  • Field: 100-110m × 64-75m
  • Ball circumference: 68-70 cm
  • Goal size: 7.32m × 2.44m
  • Average goals per match: 2.7
  • Top player speed: 36+ km/h

Judo

Individual Sport

Rules

  • Match duration: 4 minutes (senior men)
  • Objective: Throw opponent on back (Ippon)
  • No strikes or kicks allowed
  • Points: Ippon, Waza-ari, Yuko
  • Groundwork (Ne-waza) submissions permitted

Strategy

  • Gripping techniques (Kumi-kata)
  • Balance breaking (Kuzushi)
  • Timing and momentum exploitation
  • Transition from standing to ground
  • Counter-attack techniques

Facts & History

  • Created by Jigoro Kano in Japan (1882)
  • Olympic sport since 1964 (Tokyo)
  • Means "gentle way" in Japanese
  • Belt system: White to black (dan ranks)
  • Over 20 million practitioners globally

Key Tournaments

  • Olympic Games
  • World Judo Championships
  • IJF World Tour
  • Grand Slam tournaments
  • Continental Championships

Statistics

  • Mat size: 10m × 10m
  • Weight categories: 7 for men, 7 for women
  • 67 official throwing techniques
  • Golden Score: Sudden death overtime
  • Judogi (uniform) weight: 650-750g

Legendary Players

Teddy Riner

France | Heavyweight

Ryoko Tani

Japan | Extra Lightweight

Yasuhiro Yamashita

Japan | Open Weight

Tennis

Individual Sport

Rules

  • Scoring: Love, 15, 30, 40, Game
  • Must win by 2 games/points margin
  • Best of 3 or 5 sets format
  • Ball must bounce once before return
  • Serve alternates every game

Strategy

  • Serve and volley vs baseline game
  • Court positioning and angles
  • Spin variation (topspin, slice, flat)
  • Mental endurance and focus
  • Surface adaptation (clay, grass, hard)

Facts & History

  • Originated in 12th century France
  • Modern rules established in 1874
  • Wimbledon is oldest tournament (1877)
  • Olympic sport since 1988
  • Grand Slam concept since 1933

Key Tournaments

  • Australian Open
  • French Open (Roland Garros)
  • Wimbledon
  • US Open
  • ATP/WTA Finals

Statistics

  • Court dimensions: 23.77m × 8.23m (singles)
  • Net height: 91.4cm at center
  • Ball diameter: 6.54-6.86 cm
  • Fastest serve: 263 km/h (Sam Groth)
  • Longest match: 11 hours 5 minutes

Legendary Players

Roger Federer

Switzerland | All-Court

Serena Williams

USA | Baseline Power

Rafael Nadal

Spain | Clay Specialist

Motorcycle Racing

Individual Sport

Rules

  • Riders compete on closed circuits
  • Classes: MotoGP, Moto2, Moto3
  • Points awarded by finishing position
  • Strict safety gear requirements
  • Penalties for dangerous riding

Strategy

  • Tire management throughout race
  • Aerodynamic body positioning
  • Overtaking zones identification
  • Braking point optimization
  • Weather adaptation (wet/dry setup)

Facts & History

  • First race held in 1894 (Paris-Rouen)
  • MotoGP established in 1949
  • Bikes reach speeds over 350 km/h
  • Electronic aids regulate performance
  • Isle of Man TT: Most dangerous race

Key Championships

  • MotoGP World Championship
  • Superbike World Championship
  • Isle of Man TT
  • Dakar Rally
  • Motocross World Championship

Statistics

  • MotoGP engine: 1000cc 4-stroke
  • Top speed: 356.7 km/h
  • Race distance: ~120 km
  • Lean angle: Up to 64 degrees
  • G-force in corners: 1.5-2G

Volleyball

Team Sport

Rules

  • 6 players per team on court
  • Best of 5 sets (first to 25 points)
  • Maximum 3 touches per side
  • Players rotate positions clockwise
  • Rally scoring system (point every play)

Strategy

  • 5-1 or 6-2 rotation systems
  • Block formation (single, double, triple)
  • Serve receive patterns
  • Quick attacks and combination plays
  • Defensive positioning (perimeter, rotational)

Facts & History

  • Invented by William Morgan in 1895
  • Originally called "Mintonette"
  • Olympic sport since 1964
  • Beach volleyball added in 1996
  • Over 800 million players globally

Key Tournaments

  • FIVB World Championship
  • Olympic Games
  • FIVB World Cup
  • Nations League
  • Continental Championships

Statistics

  • Court: 18m × 9m
  • Net height: 2.43m (men), 2.24m (women)
  • Ball circumference: 65-67 cm
  • Fastest spike: 132 km/h
  • Average rally length: 5-7 seconds

Legendary Players

Golf

Individual Sport

Rules

  • Play ball from tee to hole in fewest strokes
  • Standard round: 18 holes
  • Ball must be played as it lies
  • Maximum 14 clubs allowed
  • Penalties for out-of-bounds and hazards

Strategy

  • Course management and club selection
  • Wind and terrain reading
  • Green reading for putting
  • Risk vs reward decision making
  • Mental game and focus maintenance

Facts & History

  • Modern golf originated in 15th century Scotland
  • First 18-hole course: St Andrews (1764)
  • Olympic sport (returned 2016)
  • Over 34,000 golf courses worldwide
  • Ryder Cup: Prestigious team competition

Major Tournaments

  • The Masters
  • U.S. Open
  • The Open Championship
  • PGA Championship
  • Ryder Cup

Statistics

  • Par range: 3-5 strokes per hole
  • Ball diameter: 42.67 mm minimum
  • Longest drive: 515 yards (Mike Austin)
  • Hole diameter: 108 mm
  • Average course length: 6,500-7,000 yards

Chess

Individual Sport

Rules

  • 64 squares, 16 pieces per player
  • Objective: Checkmate opponent's king
  • Each piece has unique movement
  • Special moves: Castling, En passant
  • Draw conditions: Stalemate, repetition

Strategy

  • Opening theory and preparation
  • Control center of the board
  • Piece development and coordination
  • Pawn structure optimization
  • Endgame technique mastery

Facts & History

  • Originated in India (6th century)
  • Modern rules standardized by 1880s
  • First World Champion: Wilhelm Steinitz (1886)
  • Over 600 million players worldwide
  • Recognized as sport by IOC

Key Tournaments

  • World Chess Championship
  • Candidates Tournament
  • Chess Olympiad
  • Tata Steel Chess Tournament
  • Norway Chess

Statistics

  • Possible positions: 10^120
  • Average game length: 40 moves
  • Longest game: 269 moves
  • ELO rating system measures skill
  • Highest rating: 2882 (Magnus Carlsen)

Billiards

Individual Sport

Rules

  • Variants: Pool, Snooker, Carom
  • Pool: Pocket all designated balls
  • Snooker: Points for potting in sequence
  • Fouls result in opponent's turn
  • Cue ball must strike object ball first

Strategy

  • Position play and cue ball control
  • Break patterns and shot selection
  • Safety play to limit opponent
  • Angle calculations and spin application
  • Pattern recognition and planning

Facts & History

  • Evolved from lawn games in 15th century
  • Snooker invented in India (1875)
  • Pool popular in USA since 1800s
  • Professional circuits established 1900s
  • Over 35 million players worldwide

Key Tournaments

  • World Snooker Championship
  • Mosconi Cup (Pool)
  • U.S. Open Nine-ball
  • UK Championship (Snooker)
  • World Pool Masters

Statistics

  • Snooker table: 12ft × 6ft
  • Pool table: 9ft × 4.5ft (standard)
  • Cue ball diameter: 52.5mm (snooker)
  • Maximum break (snooker): 147
  • Pocket size: 85mm (snooker)

Legendary Players

Badminton

Individual Sport

Rules

  • Rally scoring to 21 points
  • Best of 3 games format
  • Shuttlecock must be hit before ground
  • Singles and doubles variants
  • Service alternates on point win

Strategy

  • Court positioning and movement
  • Deceptive shot selection
  • Net play and drop shots
  • Stamina and speed management
  • Smash power vs placement

Facts & History

  • Originated in British India (1870s)
  • Named after Badminton House (England)
  • Olympic sport since 1992
  • Fastest racquet sport (smash: 493 km/h)
  • Over 220 million active players

Key Tournaments

  • BWF World Championships
  • All England Open
  • Olympic Games
  • Thomas Cup (Men's Team)
  • Uber Cup (Women's Team)

Statistics

  • Court: 13.4m × 6.1m (doubles)
  • Net height: 1.55m at edges
  • Shuttlecock weight: 4.74-5.50g
  • Fastest smash: 493 km/h
  • Longest rally: 108 strokes

Swimming

Individual Sport

Rules

  • Four strokes: Freestyle, Backstroke, Breaststroke, Butterfly
  • Races from 50m to 1500m
  • Swimmers must touch wall on turns
  • False start results in disqualification
  • Lane assignment by qualifying times

Strategy

  • Pace management across distance
  • Breathing rhythm optimization
  • Turn efficiency and underwaters
  • Stroke technique refinement
  • Race strategy (negative split, even pace)

Facts & History

  • Ancient practice dating back millennia
  • Modern Olympic sport since 1896
  • Women's events added in 1912
  • FINA founded in 1908
  • Over 100 Olympic swimming events

Key Tournaments

  • Olympic Games
  • FINA World Championships
  • World Cup Series
  • Commonwealth Games
  • Pan Pacific Championships

Statistics

  • Olympic pool: 50m × 25m
  • Lane width: 2.5m
  • Water temperature: 25-28°C
  • World record 50m free: 20.91s (men)
  • Depth requirement: 2m minimum

Athletics (Track & Field)

Individual Sport

Rules

  • Track events: Sprints, middle, long distance
  • Field events: Jumps and throws
  • Lanes assigned for races up to 400m
  • Three attempts for field events (six for finals)
  • False start = disqualification

Strategy

  • Pacing and energy distribution
  • Starting block technique
  • Wind utilization in field events
  • Lane positioning tactics
  • Mental preparation and visualization

Facts & History

  • Ancient Olympics (776 BC) featured running
  • Modern athletics since 1896 Olympics
  • Jesse Owens' 1936 Olympic dominance
  • IAAF founded in 1912 (now World Athletics)
  • Most universal sporting discipline

Key Tournaments

  • Olympic Games
  • World Athletics Championships
  • Diamond League
  • Continental Championships
  • World Indoor Championships

Statistics

  • Standard track: 400m oval
  • 100m WR: 9.58s (Usain Bolt)
  • High jump WR: 2.45m (Javier Sotomayor)
  • Long jump WR: 8.95m (Mike Powell)
  • Marathon distance: 42.195 km
